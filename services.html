Common questions

Everything you need to know.

  • It starts with a free 30-minute call. You tell us about your business and what you want a new website to do for you. There's no obligation, no pressure, and no sales pitch — if we're not the right fit, we'll say so honestly and point you somewhere better.
  • A real conversation. We look at your current site together (if you have one), talk about the customers you want to reach, and discuss what would actually move the needle. By the end of the call, you'll have a clear sense of what we'd build and roughly what it would cost.
  • We don't publish pricing because every business is different — a one-page site for a new bakery and a multi-page site for a contractor with a service area aren't the same project. After our free call, we'll send you a written quote within 48 hours with a single agreed price. No hourly billing, no surprise invoices.
  • Most small-business sites are live within 2–4 weeks of kickoff, depending on how quickly we can collect content and feedback from you. We agree on a timeline before we start, and we stick to it. No three-month agency runarounds.
  • Honestly, it depends on what's there. If your current site has a solid foundation, sometimes a focused refresh is all you need. If it's built on a clunky template or page-builder, rebuilding is usually faster and cheaper than untangling the old one. We'll tell you straight on the call.
  • Every site we build is set up properly from day one — the right page structure, clean code, mobile-friendly layout, and your Google Business Profile connected. That gives you a real chance of showing up when local customers search for what you do. SEO isn't magic, but a properly-built site is the foundation everything else stands on.
  • Small, owner-run businesses — contractors, cafes, clinics, salons, trades, professional services, local shops. If you're a real person trying to bring in real customers from your local area, we're probably a good fit. If you're a large enterprise or a venture-backed startup, we're probably not.
  • Three things, in plain terms: clear messaging that tells visitors what you do and why they should care; obvious next steps on every page (call, book, message); and a site that loads fast and looks trustworthy on the phone. None of that is fancy — it just gets done properly, which most sites don't.
